How Millennials and Gen Z are Reshaping the Future of Work: The Future Workforce Report

Millennials and Gen Z are the future, so it’s no wonder that they’re shaping how work gets done. By 2028, these generations will make up 58 percent of the workforce. And they’re thinking ahead: Millennials and Gen Z managers are three times more likely than prior generations to prioritize future workforce planning.“We know already that the most in-demand professionals place high value on flexibility,” Forbes report.Millennial and Gen Z managers are reshaping the workforce in a multitude of ways. The Future Workforce Report also found they;

  • Support remote work.
  • Embrace an agile future.
  • Recognize the need for reskilling.

The future of work won't be about college degrees, it will be about job skills

The future of work won't be about degrees. More and more, it'll be about skills. And no one school, whether it be Harvard, General Assembly or Udacity, can ever insulate us from the unpredictability of technological progression and disruption. As a leader of a technology company and former head of engineering, I've hired many programmers during my career. And what matters to me is not whether someone has a computer science degree but how well they can think and how well they can code. In fact, among the top 20 fastest-growing skills on latest Skills Index, none require a degree.
